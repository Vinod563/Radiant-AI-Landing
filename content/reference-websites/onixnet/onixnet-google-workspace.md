## Content Writing Patterns

### Product Service Page Headline Formula
```
"[Service Category] From [Credential/Partnership]"
```
- Combines what they offer with who they are
- Credential in the headline itself — builds trust before the user even reads body copy

### Messaging Approach
- **Transformation-led:** "Transform businesses" — not "implement tools"
- **Dual benefit:** Collaboration + Cloud Productivity — two outcomes, one solution
- **Reseller credential:** Partnership status as a trust qualifier
- **Benefits over features:** Enhanced collaboration > "Google Workspace deployment"

### Content Structure
1. **Hero:** Service name + credential + benefit statement
2. **Why this matters:** Business challenges this solves
3. **Service components:** What's included (implementation, migration, support)
4. **Benefits grid:** 4-6 benefit cards with icons
5. **Proof:** Client testimonials or case study links
6. **CTA:** "Get started" or "Talk to an expert"

### Key Takeaway for Radiant
- Product/platform pages should lead with the client's transformation, not the tool
- Include the partnership/credential in the headline — it builds instant trust
- Frame capabilities as "services" not "products" — services imply ongoing value
- Always connect the platform to a business outcome (productivity, collaboration, cost)
