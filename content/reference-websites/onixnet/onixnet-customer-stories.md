## Page Overview

**Meta:** "See how organizations across industries partner with Onix to solve complex challenges, drive cloud innovation, and accelerate digital transformation."

## Content Writing Patterns

### Customer Stories Page Headline Formula
```
"Customer Success Stories With [Solution Category] [Company]"
```
- "Success Stories" not "Case Studies" — warmer, more outcome-focused
- Links stories to the solution category for relevance

### Meta Description Pattern
```
"See how organizations across industries partner with [Company] to [verb 1], [verb 2], and [verb 3]."
```
- "See how" — invites exploration
- "Across industries" — signals breadth
- Three action verbs: solve + drive + accelerate — escalating intensity

### Page Structure
- **Tabbed navigation:** Filter by industry or solution area
- **Carousel layout:** Multi-row carousel with swiper navigation
- **Card format per story:**
  ```
  [Industry Tag]
  [Client/Organization Name]
  [1-line challenge description]
  [Key metric or result in bold]
  [Read Full Story →]
  ```
- **Testimonial integration:** Client quotes embedded within story cards

### Key Messaging Patterns
- Frame stories as "partnership journeys" not "project deliveries"
- Always include the challenge → approach → outcome arc
- Quantify results wherever possible (%, $, time saved)
- Use client language in headlines (their words, not yours)

### Content Structure
1. **Hero:** "See how organizations partner with us" — invitation to explore
2. **Filter/tabs:** By industry, solution type, or company size
3. **Story cards grid:** 3-column responsive layout
4. **Featured story:** One expanded story with full metrics
5. **Testimonial section:** Client quotes carousel
6. **CTA:** "Start your success story"

### Key Takeaway for Radiant
- Call them "Success Stories" not "Case Studies" — warmer and more outcome-focused
- The page meta should use three escalating verbs: solve → drive → accelerate
- Every story card needs a quantified result visible without clicking
- Use "partnership" language throughout — clients collaborated, they weren't "served"
- Filter by industry is essential — buyers want to see their sector represented
- End with "Start your success story" — makes the reader the protagonist
