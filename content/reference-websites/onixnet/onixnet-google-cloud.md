## Page Overview
**Positioning:** "Trusted Google Cloud Strategy Partner" with "20+ Years of Excellence"
**Breadcrumb:** Home > Partners > Google Cloud

## Content Writing Patterns

### Partnership Page Headline Formula
```
"Trusted [Partner Name] [Tier] Partner" + "[N]+ Years of Excellence"
```
- "Trusted" as the lead qualifier — trust is the primary buying criterion for partnerships
- Partner tier name included for specificity
- Tenure as a credibility multiplier

### How They Present Partnerships
- **Trust-first:** "Trusted" appears before any capability description
- **Tenure signal:** "20+ Years" — longevity = reliability
- **Tier/certification visibility:** Partnership levels prominently displayed
- **Joint value prop:** Not just "we're partners" but "here's what that means for you"

### Content Structure
1. **Hero:** Partnership credential + tenure
2. **What this means for you:** Client-facing benefits of the partnership
3. **Certifications grid:** Visual display of certification badges
4. **Joint solutions:** Services enabled by the partnership
5. **Case studies:** Clients served through this partnership
6. **CTA:** "Leverage our partnership for your transformation"

### Key Takeaway for Radiant
- Partnership pages should answer "Why should I care?" not just "Who are your partners?"
- Lead with trust signals (tenure, certifications, awards)
- Frame partnerships as client benefits: "Our AWS Advanced Tier means you get..."
- Use certification badges as visual trust anchors — they scan faster than text
- Joint case studies are the strongest proof: "Together with [Partner], we delivered..."
