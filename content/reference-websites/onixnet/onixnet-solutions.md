## Content Writing Patterns

### Headline Formula (Solutions Page)
```
"Empower your [audience] with [solution category]—[benefit 1], [benefit 2], [benefit 3], and [benefit 4]."
```
- Starts with empowerment/transformation verb
- Lists 3-4 specific outcomes separated by commas
- Em dash after the category to bridge to benefits

### Service Description Pattern
- **Outcome-first:** What the client achieves, not what Onix does
- **Benefit clusters:** Group related capabilities into themed bundles
- **Tab-based exploration:** Let users self-select their area of interest
- **Each service links to:** deeper detail page + CTA

### CTA Strategy
- **Primary:** "Connect with experts today" — positions team as accessible experts
- **Secondary:** "Learn more" — content discovery path
- **Tertiary:** "Get started" — for ready buyers
- CTAs escalate in commitment as user scrolls deeper

### Content Hierarchy
1. **Hero:** Umbrella value proposition covering all solutions
2. **Solution tabs:** Each category as a selectable tab
3. **Per-tab content:** Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
4. **Cross-sell:** Related solutions suggested at bottom
5. **Social proof:** Testimonials or case study snippets per solution

### Key Takeaway for Radiant
- Don't list services as a flat grid — use tabs or progressive disclosure
- Each service section should follow: Problem → Approach → Metric → CTA
- The solutions overview page is a routing page — guide users to depth
- "Connect with experts" is stronger than "Contact Us"
