## Content Writing Patterns

### AI/ML Page Headline Formula
```
"[Company]: AI and ML Solutions to Drive [Business Outcome]"
```
- Company name first (brand anchoring)
- "Drive" as action verb (momentum, agency)
- Business outcome, not technical capability

### How They Position AI Services
- **Tailored solutions:** Not one-size-fits-all — customized to business context
- **Efficiency + Productivity:** Twin outcomes always mentioned together
- **Business innovation framing:** AI as innovation driver, not cost cutter
- **Transformation language:** "Transform your business" — not "implement AI"

### Page Elements
- Logo carousel (client trust signals)
- Grid-based logo galleries (partner ecosystem)
- Interactive forms (multiple CTA touchpoints)
- Multiple CTA button variations per section

### Content Structure
1. **Hero:** AI/ML + business outcome headline
2. **Problem statement:** Why businesses need AI now
3. **Solution overview:** Capability areas (3-4 pillars)
4. **Use cases:** Industry-specific AI applications
5. **Proof:** Client logos, case study references
6. **CTA:** Expert consultation offer

### Key Takeaway for Radiant
- AI pages should lead with business outcomes, not technology
- Use "tailored" and "customized" — buyers fear generic AI solutions
- Pair every AI capability with an efficiency metric
- Include trust signals (logos, certifications) on AI pages — trust is critical here
- Frame AI as innovation, not automation (more aspirational, less threatening)
